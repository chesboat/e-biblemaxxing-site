Privacy Policy

Effective July 9, 2026 · Applies to the Biblemaxxing iOS app
The whole policy in one sentence: your reading stays on your device; the only thing that ever leaves it is anonymous subscription status. There is no account, no ad network, and no tracking. What you read, when you read, and which apps you lock are never collected. The one exception is purchases: subscription receipts are processed under an anonymous identifier so Premium can unlock — nothing else.

What we collect

Your reading and locking data: nothing. All Scripture text (public-domain translations) ships inside the app and reading works fully offline. Your plan, streaks, and history never leave your device.

Purchases: anonymous subscription status only. When you start a trial or subscribe, the App Store receipt and subscription status (active, trial, expired) are processed by RevenueCat, our subscription manager, under a random anonymous identifier. This never includes your name, email, Apple ID, payment details, or anything about your reading. Apple's privacy label for the app reflects exactly this: purchase history and a device identifier, not linked to your identity, never used for tracking.

Screen Time & app blocking

Biblemaxxing uses Apple's Screen Time frameworks (Family Controls, Managed Settings, and Device Activity) to shield the apps you choose, with your explicit permission. Two things are worth knowing about how Apple designed this:

  • Your app selections are given to us as opaque tokens — Biblemaxxing cannot see which apps you chose, and neither can we.
  • All blocking and unblocking is enforced by iOS, on your device. No information about your app usage is available to us, transmitted anywhere, or stored anywhere except your device.

Your reading data

Your reading plan, daily portion, streaks, milestones, and reading history are stored locally on your device (in the app's private container). They are never uploaded, synced, or shared. Deleting the app deletes them; you can also reset them anytime in Profile → Reset reading progress.

Notifications

If you enable the daily reminder, notifications are scheduled locally on your device. No push-notification service is used, because there is no server to push from.

Children

Biblemaxxing is not directed at children under 13. Because the app collects no personal information from anyone, it collects none from children either.

Third parties

Exactly one: RevenueCat, which validates App Store receipts and keeps your subscription unlocked across reinstalls. It receives purchase events under an anonymous identifier and nothing else — see RevenueCat's privacy policy. Payment itself is handled entirely by the App Store under Apple's privacy policy — we never see your payment details.

If you discover Biblemaxxing through an Apple Search Ads ad, Apple's AdServices framework provides an anonymous attribution token (which campaign the install came from — never who you are). There are no ad networks, analytics brokers, or social SDKs in the app, and nothing is ever used for cross-app tracking.

Changes to this policy

If Biblemaxxing ever adds a feature that touches data (for example, optional iCloud sync), this policy will be updated first, the effective date will change, and the app's App Store privacy label will be updated to match.
